This was a team assignment, which could range from 4-6 people working on a single learning application. Since this was a mass group project, there were aspect that I did not contribute to. The entire project will be discussed in Design and Development, but the exact parts I worked directly on will be discussed in Final Product.

For my team’s learning product, we elected to create and interactive play maker for football teams. It was developed using QtCreator and C++ per our professor’s instruction. This assignment was the final for CS3505/Software Practices II.

Design and Development

For this assignment, we did a scrum approach to our project. We had two days to discuss prior to the beginning of the programming phase. D uring the programming phase, we aimed for 3 meetings a week. During these meetings, I would transcribe everyone’s accomplishments, obstacles, and goals. In place of meetings, we occasionally did message check-ins and I’d review everyone’s progress on the GitHub daily.

Since all of our schedules were very different and we wanted to avoid having to meet anymore than necessary with finals approaching, we split our project into three parts: the playmaker, the play viewer, and the quiz portion. This made it easier to split the work. This ensured we weren’t repeating work that someone else may have done, but not yet pushed.

We also created a list of most to least important tasks. For everyone’s roles during the week, we would have 1-2 required tasks, 1-2 quality of life tasks, and 1 polish task. For example, for our playmaker, we had additional time during the second week of the project, so we wanted to try adding animation. A required task would be a pause and play button, a QOL task would be a full-screen button, and a polish task would be interpolating frames.
